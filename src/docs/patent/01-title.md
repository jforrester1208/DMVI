# Dynamic Market Value Insurance System and Method

**Version:** 0.1

---

## Working Title

Dynamic Market Value Insurance System and Method for Determining Insurance Premiums and Claim Payments for Collectible Assets Using Time-Weighted Market Valuation.

---

## Field of the Invention

The present invention relates generally to property and casualty insurance and, more particularly, to computerized systems and methods for determining insurance premiums and settling claims for collectible and other market-valued property whose value fluctuates over the policy term.

## Purpose of this Disclosure

This disclosure describes a system in which:

- A Deposit Premium is calculated using a Scheduled Value established at policy inception.
- The insurer periodically determines the current Market Value of the insured property using one or more approved valuation methodologies.
- The Final Earned Premium is determined by applying the applicable premium rate to the Time-Weighted Average Market Value of the insured property during the policy period.
- The Deposit Premium is credited against the Final Earned Premium, resulting in either an additional premium due from the insured or a return premium payable by the insurer.
- Covered losses are settled using the Market Value immediately preceding the Date of Loss.
- The insured is provided an auditable explanation of the valuation used in determining claim payment.

The invention seeks to more closely align insurance premiums and claim settlements with actual market values while improving transparency, consistency, and fairness for both insurers and insureds.
