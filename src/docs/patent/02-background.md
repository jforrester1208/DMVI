# Introduction

The insurance industry has long recognized the challenge of insuring property whose market value changes over time. Traditional insurance products generally assume that the insured value remains reasonably stable throughout the policy period. While this assumption is acceptable for many classes of personal property, it becomes increasingly problematic for collectible assets whose market values may fluctuate significantly due to changing market conditions.

Luxury watches provide a representative example of this problem. The secondary market for certain watch models has experienced substantial appreciation and depreciation over relatively short periods of time. Similar valuation volatility exists for numerous other collectible assets including fine jewelry, coins, fine art, trading cards, musical instruments, luxury handbags, rare books, wine collections, and other specialty property.

---

# Traditional Insurance Methods

Existing insurance products generally employ one or more of the following valuation methods:

- Scheduled Value
- Agreed Value
- Stated Value
- Replacement Cost
- Actual Cash Value

Each approach provides certain advantages but also introduces limitations when applied to property whose market value changes materially during the policy period.

---

# Scheduled Value Policies

Under a Scheduled Value policy, the insured declares a value for each insured item at policy inception. Premium is calculated using the scheduled amount, and coverage is generally based upon that value subject to the policy provisions.

The scheduled value often remains unchanged throughout the policy period unless the insured requests an endorsement modifying the schedule.

---

# Market Value Changes

During the policy period, the market value of collectible assets may increase or decrease due to numerous factors including:

- Supply and demand
- Manufacturer production changes
- Discontinued models
- Economic conditions
- Currency fluctuations
- Collector demand
- Auction activity
- Market speculation
- Scarcity
- Historical significance

As a result, the value declared at policy inception may no longer represent the current market value at the time of loss.

---

# Existing Administrative Practices

When market values change, insureds may request endorsements increasing or decreasing scheduled values.

Many insureds, however:

- are unaware of market changes;
- delay requesting policy changes;
- intentionally postpone premium increases;
- or simply forget to update scheduled values.

Even when insureds diligently monitor market values and promptly notify the insurer of changes, the insurer must typically process one or more policy endorsements reflecting the revised scheduled values. Such endorsements may require underwriting review, policy administration, premium recalculation, billing adjustments, issuance of revised policy documents, and related administrative processing.

Accordingly, maintaining scheduled values that accurately reflect current market conditions may require repeated policy modifications throughout the policy period, increasing administrative expense for both insurers and insureds.

---

# Claims Administration

When a covered loss occurs, insurers frequently must determine the current value of the lost property.

This process may involve:

- obtaining market information;
- consulting valuation services;
- reviewing comparable sales;
- evaluating supporting documentation;
- applying policy provisions;
- resolving valuation disputes.

The process may require additional time and expense before the claim can be resolved.

---

# Need for Improvement

Accordingly, there exists a need for an insurance system capable of:

- more closely aligning earned premium with actual market exposure;
- determining claim payments using objective valuation methodologies;
- providing greater transparency to insureds;
- reducing reliance upon manual policy endorsements;
- maintaining an auditable valuation history;
- accommodating multiple valuation methodologies;
- supporting insurer-specific business rules; and
- improving consistency in premium determination and claim settlement.
