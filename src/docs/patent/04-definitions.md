# Definitions

**Version:** 0.1

---

# Purpose

Unless the context clearly requires otherwise, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below. These definitions are intended to promote consistency throughout this disclosure and are not intended to limit the scope of the invention.

---

# Covered Property

Property insured under a policy implementing the disclosed system.

Covered Property may include, without limitation:

- Luxury watches
- Jewelry
- Fine art
- Coins
- Currency
- Trading cards
- Musical instruments
- Luxury handbags
- Wine collections
- Precious metals
- Collectibles
- Other tangible or intangible property capable of valuation.

---

# Scheduled Value

The value assigned to Covered Property at policy inception or upon subsequent endorsement.

The Scheduled Value serves as the basis for determining the Deposit Premium but does not necessarily determine either the Final Earned Premium or the amount payable upon a covered loss.

---

# Deposit Premium

The premium calculated at policy inception using the Scheduled Value.

The Deposit Premium represents an initial premium deposit that is reconciled against the Final Earned Premium following expiration or termination of the policy period.

---

# Market Value

The value assigned to Covered Property using one or more insurer-approved valuation methodologies as of a specified date.

Market Value may be determined using any combination of:

- Market observations
- Comparable sales
- Dealer quotations
- Auction results
- Internal valuation models
- Third-party valuation services
- Proprietary valuation algorithms
- Manual review
- Other valuation techniques selected by the insurer.

The disclosed invention is independent of any particular valuation methodology.

---

# Valuation Methodology

The process, algorithm, business rules, data sources, models, assumptions, and procedures used by the insurer to determine Market Value.

Different insurers may utilize different valuation methodologies without departing from the principles of the disclosed invention.

---

# Valuation Event

Any event resulting in the determination or revision of Market Value.

Valuation Events may occur:

- Periodically
- On scheduled intervals
- Upon significant market activity
- Following underwriting review
- Upon policy modification
- At claim time
- Or at any other time determined appropriate by the insurer.

---

# Valuation Reference Date

The date and time designated under the applicable insurance policy for determining the Market Value used for a particular insurance function, including but not limited to premium determination, claim settlement, underwriting, or policy administration.

For claim settlement purposes, the Valuation Reference Date will ordinarily be the Date of Loss. However, the insurer may designate an alternative reference date or time in accordance with the terms of the applicable policy, provided that such designation is applied consistently and in accordance with the insurer's approved valuation methodology.

The disclosed invention is not limited to any particular Valuation Reference Date and may accommodate different insurer business rules governing the selection and application of such date.

---

# Time-Weighted Average Market Value

The weighted average of one or more Market Values determined during the policy period, where each Market Value is weighted according to the period of time during which it remained applicable.

The specific mathematical calculation may vary according to insurer business rules.

---

# Final Earned Premium

The premium determined by applying the applicable premium rate to the Time-Weighted Average Market Value during the policy period.

The Deposit Premium shall be credited against the Final Earned Premium.

---

# Date of Loss

The date and time at which a covered loss occurs, as determined under the applicable insurance policy.

---

# Valuation History

The chronological record of Market Values determined during the policy period, together with supporting valuation information, effective dates, and associated metadata maintained by the insurer.

---

# Transparency Report

Information made available to the insured describing how a Market Value was determined, including, where applicable:

- Effective valuation date
- Applicable valuation methodology
- Supporting market information
- Calculation details
- Other information determined appropriate by the insurer.

---

# Automated Valuation

The determination of Market Value using software, business rules, algorithms, valuation models, or other automated processes, with or without human review.

Automated Valuation does not require that every valuation be determined without human involvement.
